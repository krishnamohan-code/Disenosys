Every day, you wake up and sit in front of your laptop to start job hunting. How many jobs do you apply every day? I guess you don’t even count anymore!

Have you ever thought about why you are not getting shortlisted for the interviews?

Ok, even if you do attend interviews,why are you not getting hired?

Amid all this chaos, the only relief you have is, that so many mechanical engineering graduates are doing the same!

Let me tell you something real…

You are in demand!

The Bureau of Labor Statistics has projected that Mechanical Engineers employment rate will go up by 9% by 2026. Driving this growth are innovations within the automation, medical device, biotechnology and aerospace industries.

So why aren’t you hired yet?

time to face another reality…

Who get mechanical engineering jobs in India?

  • If you belong to IITs, Top NITs and other prestigious Institutes, then answer is a big yes.
  • If you belong to an average government engineering college, then you have job opportunities on an average 5/10.
  • Now, the last case. If you are a student of an unknown private college, You will get very fewer opportunities in your college(1/10). But Off Campuses are still hope. Selection ratio is very low in off-campus.
  • There are PSUs ( NTPC, BHEL, IOCL, BPCL, HPC, HPCL, ONGC, Coal India, SAIL) who recruits based on Gate result and their interview.



    • Why them and not you?

    Your school friend from an IIT is already working in some OEM but you are still searching for a job… let’s break it down.

    1. Name of the college does play a role when it comes to top companies visiting a college for campus selection.

    2. Though you have the same skill sets, chances of you getting hired are less due to the difference in the quality of education one receives in top colleges.

    3. Students from top colleges have many internships and projects added to their resume than an average college student.

    So, are there hopes for me to get hired in a core company?

    Hell yes!

    But... How?

    1. The domain you want to enter

    Did you take the right career guidance from a technical person who has already worked in the industry for years?

    2. Check your resume.

    Have you done enough projects and internships in the domain you want to get the job?

    3. Required Practical knowledge

    Have you worked on some real-world projects in the specific domain to understand it in depth?
