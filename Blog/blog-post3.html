SKILL GAP BETWEEN MECHANICAL GRADUATES AND INDUSTRY REQUIREMENTS

  • Nishtha Tiwari
  • 26 Aug 2020

With the Industry 4.0 knocking at the door, there is a need to upgrade skills to match the requirements of the new technological world. India's student population is estimated to increase more than 600 mn in the next couple of years. Less than half of the population is employable. It is evident that India has miserably failed to invest in human capital. "In 2018, with a score of 0.44 on a scale of 0 to 1, India ranked 115th in the Human Capital Index (HCI), much lower than the average global score."

"The skill gap in India remains a major problem impeding the growth potentials of the economy. The International Labour Organization (ILO) believes that India is staring at a 29 million skill-deficit by 2030. Accenture’s projection is that the skill gap in India will cause a loss of $1.97 trillion in terms of gross domestic product promised by investment in intelligent technologies over the 10 years if it is not controlled.", writes indiaemployerforum.org. It is not only going to affect the economy of the country but also hamper the standard of living for many.

It is assumed by the employers in such cases, that higher the education, higher is the skill set. More qualifications lead to more job opportunities. According to a report of Skill India 2019, the employability rate has increased in five years from 37% to 47.38%. It was a relief to see that there is still hope.

Companies are making use of technical education platforms to train the freshers and they continue to skill-up their senior employees. This is necessary to meet the daily challenges at the workplace. Without getting comfortable with the new ways, possibly the companies will never survive. Thus, the corporates are now investing in human capital to keep them as assets and not turn them into liability.

"Little above half (56%) of the labour market is dominated by people who are classified at Level 2 skills, while 30% constitute skill Level 1 type. Nearly 11% of the population can be classified at skill Level 3, while the smallest share is that of skill Level 4. Slightly more than over half of skill Level 1 individuals are in the 15-35 years age group, whereas this group constitutes to about 40% for other skill level types. Over one-third of skill Level 4 individuals belong in the 36-45 years age group, which is higher than that for other skill levels. It is not surprising that higher skill level individuals reside in urban areas—a mere 26% of skill Level 2 individuals reside in urban areas.”, writes Rajesh Shukla, director and CEO -People Research on India’s Consumer Economy (PRICE).

To meet the demands from now on, the engineers themselves will have to acknowledge their lack of skill set and work towards meeting the job requirements. The companies are not going to look back for you, you will have to be one step ahead of them to lead the future. It is easy to say than it is to do.

Government is also realising the need to grow Indian talent pool volume by helping them learn at various platforms like eSkillIndia , NASSCOM, SkillsBuild,NPTEL, SWAYAM, etc. Not all the courses though are industry specific and guarantee you a strong resume for your dream Job.

But, the future is still quite promising for mechanical and automobile engineering. "According to a report in 2016 by the global consultancy firm Deloitte, India is expected to grow manifold in manufacturing by the year 2020. As per its report on Global Manufacturing Competitiveness index, India will move up 6 positions to be at 5th position. From its report, Deloitte claims that India will strengthen its hold in the manufacturing sector and thus, there would be numerous new opportunities for mechanical engineers in India. However, the compensation packages of these mechanical engineers are going to depend on the skills and expertise, which is why it has become extremely important that the skill gap be bridged to make the new graduates aware of the latest tools as well as technologies in the industries.", as per https://www.mahindraecolecentrale.edu.in/blogs/scope-of-mechanical-engineering-in-india-whats-next
