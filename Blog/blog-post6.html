Career Opportunities for Plastic-Trims Engineers!

  • Amreen Rushda
  • 26 Aug 2020

According to the National Investment Promotion and facilitation Agency, "India is expected to be the world's third-largest automotive market in terms of volume by 2026.

The Industry currently manufactures 26 mn vehicles, of which 4.7 mn are exported. India holds a strong place in the international heavy vehicles arena as it is the largest tractor manufacturer, second-largest bus manufacturer and third largest heavy trucks manufacturer in the world.

In April-March 2020, overall automobile exports recorded an increase of 2.95%. Passenger Vehicles exports marginally rose by 0.17% and Two-wheelers exports registered a growth of 7.30% in April-March 2020 over the same period last year."

The people driving this change and growth are engineers working day and night, and the consumers who consistently demand better quality and facilities. The automotive design sector is the base of car manufacturing. One cannot imagine launching a product without employing those who carve the beauty of a stylish car. Here come in the picture the Plastic-Trim Engineers. With the growth of the sector and increased manufacturing in India, one can easily make out that the opportunities in this field are immense, given you have the required set of skills which are:-

  • Good knowledge of plastic-part design.
  • Application of Geometric design and tolerancing.
  • Good knowledge on the selection of materials for automotive parts and components.
  • Exposure to the manufacturing process and sheet metal operation.
  • Good knowledge of Vehicles regulations.
  • CAD, CATIA software experience.
  • Conceptual thinking.
  • Team leadership experience.
  • Problem-solving and decision-making skills.
  • Strong Communication skills.
  • Adaptability to the working environment.
  • Flexible to meet deadlines.

    • According to India Brand Equity Foundation, "Some of the recent/planned investments and developments in the automobile sector in India are as follows:

  • In April 2020, TVS Motor Company bought UK’s iconic sporting motorcycle brand, Norton, for a sum of about Rs 153 crore (US$ 21.89 million), making its entry into the top end (above 850cc) segment of the superbike market.
  • As of May 2019, Jaguar Land Rover (JLR) launched its locally assembled Range Rover Velar, making JLR cars more affordable by quite some margin.
  • In March 2020, Lithium Urban Technologies partnered with renewable energy solutions provider, Fourth Partner Energy, to build charging infrastructure across the country.
  • In January 2020, Tata AutoComp Systems, the auto-components arm of Tata Group entered a joint venture with Beijing-based Prestolite Electric to enter the electric vehicle (EV) components market.
  • In December 2019, Force Motors planned to invest Rs 600 crore (US$ 85.85 million) in order to develop two new models over the next two years.
  • In December 2019, Morris Garages (MG), a British automobile brand, announced plans to invest an additional Rs 3,000 crore (US$ 429.25 million) in India.
  • Audi India planned to launch nine all-new models including Sedans and SUVs along with futuristic E-tron EV by end of 2019.
  • MG Motor India planned to launch MG ZS EV electric SUV in early 2020 and have plans to launch affordable EV in the next 3-4 years.
  • BYD-Olectra, Tata Motors and Ashok Leyland will supply 5,500 electric buses for different state departments."
















    • Most of the times big companies demand 2+ years of experience in a particular field. An aspiring plastic-trim engineer should be focused to develop the above skills and take up internships in the related companies to build a strong resume. One who has worked on related projects and has some background knowledge of the work environment is always preferred for the job. The competition is tough but the requirement is only talent and hard work.

    Currently AllyGrow, Volvo group, Virtual Simutech, All Web Design, DailyCADCAM, APPL Global InfoTech, Smart Enovations like companies continued to hire plastic-trim engineers even after lockdown restrictions. There are 650 job openings in India as of now and once the industry is back in action, the workforce demand is likely to grow. And surprisingly abroad India, the number of vacancies is 2039. The numbers keep on changing but this clearly indicates all you need to have is a skill-set that you can tailor to the job requirements.

    With the wave of electrification of vehicles and retrofitting operations coming into full swing by the next year, design engineers will be a fish to catch.





















    According to a report published on marketsandmarkets.com, “BEV manufacturers are working on reducing their overall weight, which is likely to enhance the range of the electric vehicle. To reduce the weight of the vehicle automotive OEMs are focusing on metal to plastic conversion and injection moulded plastic parts. Furthermore, OEMs are also focusing on strength and safety, and cost-effective plastics with high-temperature resistive property for battery compartment area. This trend is likely to create new opportunities for the plastic supplier, which are lighter in weight and comparatively less expensive than metal components.”














    Also, the report states, “Asia accounted for a majority of share of overall plastics for electric vehicle market, in terms of volume, in 2020. It is projected to register a CAGR of 28.4% during the forecast period. China, Japan, India, and South Korea are the major countries in the region. The major companies in this region are LG Chem, Asahi Kasei, Daikin, AGC, Sumitomo Chemical Company, and JSR Corporation. In terms of geography, the Asia region has the highest sales of electric vehicles. Henceforth, Asia is the largest as well as the fastest-growing electric vehicle plastics market.”

    They will be looked for, paid well and valued. The work is never complete unless they give the car their touch and finish. The salary package for plastic trim engineers depends on various factors like their experience in the field, skills, projects they have worked on, demand in the market and the company that hires them. For beginners in the field, it may vary from 0-3.5 LPA and the experienced engineers will easily get somewhere between 5.5-8 LPA.
